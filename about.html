Who We Are

Crafting the World's Most Extraordinary Adventures

Stackly Expeditions was born on the slopes of Denali in 2003, when two certified mountain guides decided the world deserved better than cookie-cutter adventure travel. We built something different: smaller groups, deeper expertise, obsessive safety standards, and a genuine commitment to leaving every wild place better than we found it.

Two decades on, we've guided 14,000 adventurers across 87 destinations on all seven continents. Every expedition is designed from scratch — no templates, no shortcuts, no compromises.

Our Journey

Two Decades of Adventure

2003
Founded on Denali
Two UIAGM-certified guides — Marcus Webb and Priya Sharma — launch Stackly with one Denali expedition and a vision to transform adventure travel.
2007
Himalayan Expansion
Stackly launches its flagship Everest Base Camp programme and Annapurna Circuit tours. First season sells out in 72 hours.
2011
Africa & Wildlife Safaris
We open our Kilimanjaro and Serengeti operations with exclusive campsite agreements and community partnership programmes across Tanzania.
2016
Patagonia & Polar Routes
Torres del Paine and Iceland expeditions launch. Stackly becomes the first adventure operator to offer carbon-offset documentation on every booking.
2021
Carbon Neutral Declaration
Stackly achieves full carbon neutrality across all operations. We plant 10,000 trees annually and fund glacier monitoring research in Nepal.
2025
14,000 Adventurers & Beyond
We celebrate our 14,000th guided adventurer and launch seven new routes across Southeast Asia, the Amazon, and the High Arctic.

What Drives Us

Our Core Values

01
Safety Above All
No summit, no deadline, no client pressure will ever override our safety protocols. We turn back when the mountain demands it — without hesitation.
02
Radical Authenticity
We design real expeditions, not tourist experiences. Honest itineraries, transparent difficulty ratings, and zero-inflated success promises.
03
Leave It Wilder
Every Stacklyexpedition funds active conservation. We leave every route, camp, and summit cleaner than we found it — always.
04
Community Partnership
We hire locally, pay fairly, and invest in the communities whose mountains we are privileged to explore. Responsible tourism is non-negotiable.
05
Lifelong Learning
All guides complete 40+ hours of annual training. We study changing weather patterns, medical advances, and new route conditions obsessively.
06
Transformative Experience
We measure success not just in summits reached, but in perspectives shifted. Every Stackly expedition is designed to change how you see the world.
